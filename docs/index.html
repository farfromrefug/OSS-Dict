OSS-Dict

Offline Dictionary Reader

A free and open-source offline dictionary reader for Android. Fork of Aard 2 with Material Design 3, smart lookup, math rendering, bookmarks and more. No internet required.

What's inside

Everything you need in a dictionary app

OSS-Dict packs a full suite of reading and lookup tools into a beautifully crafted, completely offline experience.

Smart Lookup

Search is punctuation, diacritics and case insensitive — find what you mean, not just what you type.

Bookmarks & History

Save articles and revisit your reading history. Filter and sort by time or title, with easy bulk removal.

Dictionary Management

Load multiple dictionaries, reorder them, toggle them on/off or remove them — all without touching files.

Custom Stylesheets

Apply built-in or your own CSS stylesheets to personalize article appearance exactly to your taste.

Beautiful Math Support

Mathematical formulas rendered via MathJax — scalable, styleable and crisp on every screen.

Random Article

Tap the app logo to discover a random entry. Optionally limit random lookups to your favourite dictionaries.

Volume Button Navigation

Use hardware volume keys to scroll articles or cycle through tabs — hands-free reading made easy.

Fullscreen Mode

Immerse yourself in content with fullscreen article viewing. Pull down from the top edge to exit.

Clipboard Auto-Paste

Copied text (excluding URLs, emails and phone numbers) is automatically pasted into the search field.

External Link Sharing

Long-tap any link in an article to share it directly — perfect for Wikipedia and Wiktionary links.

Get dictionaries

Download dictionary files

OSS-Dict reads .slob dictionary files. Add your own files using Android's file picker — the app never downloads dictionaries itself. Here are the best places to find them:

Offline & Private by Design

OSS-Dict works completely offline. The INTERNET permission is required only for the local embedded web server (localhost communication) used to display content — no data is ever sent online. Articles may optionally load remote images when you allow it, but all dictionary lookups stay on-device.
